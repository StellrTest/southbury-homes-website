Seasonal Maintenance

A year round guide to caring for your home.

Regular seasonal maintenance helps protect your investment, improve energy efficiency, and keep your Southbury Home operating at its best year round.

Spring

Recover from winter and prepare for the warm months.

HVAC & Mechanical Systems

  • Test the temperature and pressure relief valve on your hot water tank (refer to the manufacturer's instructions and exercise caution, as hot water may be released).
  • Clean or replace furnace filters monthly during the heating season.
  • Inspect HRV/ERV filters every two months and clean as required.
  • Shut down, clean, and drain furnace humidifiers.
  • Open humidifier dampers only as recommended.
  • Turn on and test your air conditioning system. Service every 2–3 years.
  • Clean or replace air conditioning filters.
  • Check and clean dehumidifiers and drain lines.
  • Turn off furnace and fireplace pilot lights where applicable.

Safety Checks

  • Test smoke alarms, carbon monoxide detectors, and security systems.
  • Replace batteries as needed.

Exterior & Foundation

  • Inspect foundation walls for cracks, moisture, or leaks.
  • Ensure sump pumps are operating properly before spring thaw.
  • Verify sump pump discharge lines direct water away from the foundation.
  • Inspect exterior cladding for openings that may allow pests to enter.
  • Check eavestroughs and downspouts for blockages and proper drainage.
  • Clear drainage ditches and culverts.
  • Re level decks, steps, and walkways affected by frost movement.

Windows & Doors

  • Clean windows, screens, and hardware.
  • Replace storm windows with screens where applicable.
  • Repair or replace damaged screens.

Landscaping

  • Open outdoor hose bibs after frost danger has passed.
  • Repair and paint fences as needed.
  • Perform seasonal landscaping maintenance.
  • Fertilize young trees and shrubs if required.

Water Systems

  • Test well water quality and bacteria levels every six months.

Summer

Keep your home cool, efficient, and well ventilated.

HVAC & Ventilation

  • Maintain indoor humidity below 60%.
  • Clean or replace air conditioning filters.
  • Clean HRV/ERV filters as needed.

Plumbing

  • Inspect basement plumbing for condensation or leaks.
  • Insulate cold water pipes if necessary.
  • Check floor drains and ensure traps contain water.
  • Run water periodically in seldom used fixtures.

Interior Maintenance

  • Deep clean carpets and area rugs.
  • Vacuum bathroom exhaust fan grilles.
  • Clean dryer ducts, vents, and surrounding areas.

Windows & Doors

  • Inspect and lubricate windows, hinges, and door hardware.
  • Repair damaged paint on windows and doors.
  • Replace deteriorated caulking and weatherstripping.

Garage

  • Lubricate garage door hardware and opener components.
  • Test garage door auto reverse safety features.

Exterior & Roof

  • Inspect siding, trim, and exterior finishes.
  • Remove vegetation touching the home.
  • Inspect roof shingles, flashing, and chimney components from ground level.
  • Check chimney cap and surrounding caulking.
  • Inspect electrical service entry points and overhead lines.

Walkways & Safety

  • Repair damaged driveways, walkways, and steps.
  • Inspect all handrails and guardrails for stability.

Fall

Prepare your home and systems for the cold ahead.

Heating Systems

  • Service your furnace or heating system as recommended by the manufacturer.
  • Inspect and clean furnace components.
  • Lubricate circulating pumps where applicable.
  • Bleed hot water radiators.
  • Vacuum electric baseboard heaters.
  • Clean air ducts and return grilles.
  • Test furnace operation before cold weather arrives.
  • Replace furnace filters regularly during heating season.

Ventilation Systems

  • Inspect and clean HRV/ERV filters and cores.
  • Check ductwork for leaks or damaged insulation.
  • Test condensate drains.
  • Verify bathroom fans and range hoods are functioning properly.

Safety Checks

  • Test smoke alarms, carbon monoxide detectors, and security systems.
  • Replace batteries as needed.

Windows & Doors

  • Replace screens with storm windows.
  • Inspect weatherstripping and repair as necessary.
  • Ensure windows, skylights, and exterior doors close tightly.
  • Check garage to house doors and self closing mechanisms.

Exterior Preparation

  • Shut off and cover air conditioning units.
  • Confirm grading slopes away from the foundation.
  • Remove leaves from roofs, gutters, and downspouts.
  • Drain and store garden hoses.
  • Shut off and drain exterior hose bibs where applicable.

Water & Septic Systems

  • Test well water quality every six months.
  • Inspect septic systems and schedule pumping when required.

Landscaping

  • Store outdoor furniture.
  • Prepare gardens and landscaping for winter.
  • Protect young trees and shrubs from winter damage.

Winter

Protect against the cold and watch for moisture.

Heating & Air Quality

  • Inspect and replace furnace filters every 2–3 months.
  • Check and clean HRV/ERV filters every two months.
  • Replace humidifier evaporator pads annually.
  • Watch for signs of excess indoor humidity and condensation.

Safety Checks

  • Vacuum smoke and fire detectors monthly.
  • Test fire extinguishers and inspect pressure gauges.
  • Review family fire escape plans.
  • Ensure exterior lighting is functioning properly.

Plumbing

  • Drain sediment from the hot water tank as recommended by the manufacturer.
  • Check for dripping faucets and leaking fixtures.
  • Run water periodically in seldom used plumbing fixtures.
  • Test plumbing shut off valves.
  • Clean drains throughout the home.
  • Ensure floor drain traps contain water.

Interior Maintenance

  • Vacuum bathroom exhaust fan grilles.
  • Clean refrigerator and freezer coils and drip trays.

Exterior & Roof Care

  • Do not use salt on concrete porches, stairs, driveways, or garage floors. Use sand or concrete safe ice melters instead.
  • Inspect windows and doors for drafts and ice buildup.
  • Check attics for frost accumulation.
  • Monitor roofs for ice dams and icicles.
  • Remove snow from gas meters, appliance vents, exhaust vents, basement windows, decks, and flat roof areas.
  • Consult qualified contractors if ice buildup becomes excessive.

Electrical Safety

  • Inspect all indoor and outdoor cords, plugs, and outlets.
  • Replace damaged electrical components immediately.
